Trained as a Wildlife Conservation Biologist.

My professional background is in wildlife ecology and management, where I have spent the past 7+ years building my career. During this time, I have worked on a variety of projects researching and managing wildlife and their habitats.

Drawn to the world of
GIS & geospatial inquiry.

As I advanced in my wildlife career, I began to recognize that location was a linking factor for nearly all ecological data. This realization developed into a fascination in spatial ecology and geospatial technologies.

Leveraging my passions
for nature & conservation.

As a nature blogger, I aim to inspire and empower readers to make a positive impact on the environment and get involved in conservation efforts. I also use my platform to educate others about conservation and encourage them to take action to protect the natural world.

Continuously Improving
my Multi-Disciplinary Skillset

My diverse knowledge and competencies in a range of disciplines enables me to find creative solutions to complex problems. I work hard to develop new skills each day and actively seek out experiences to grow professionally and personally.

  • Cartographic Design

    Adept at using R, ArcGIS Pro, and ArcGIS Online to create static and dynamic maps, with a comprehensive understanding of cartographic design fundamentals.

  • Data Viz & Reporting

    Proficient in data visualization and reproducible reporting using Quarto, R Markdown, and Microsoft Word, and have a proven ability to effectively communicate data insights.

  • Web Development

    Employ front-end web development languages such as HTML and CSS to design functional and beautiful websites and interactive mapping applications.

  • Data Management

    Use programs like Microsoft Access, Microsoft SQL Server, and ArcGIS Pro to manipulate and maintain high-quality data in relational databases.

  • Project Management

    Use tools like Microsoft Teams, SharePoint, and Lists to manage complex projects and collaborate with team members efficiently.

  • Field Research Methods

    Experience using radio telemetry, standardized field sampling, and wildlife capture and marking techniques to perform well in ther field.
