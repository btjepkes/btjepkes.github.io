Formal Education

My post-secondary training and education is situated at the crossroads of wildlife ecology and geography at both the undergraduate and graduate levels. These areas of specialization resonate with me due to their comprehensive and diverse nature of study, as well as their infusion of technological solutions.

University of Missouri
2023 - Present
Masters [MA] - Geography

I am currently studying at the University of Missouri in pursuit of my Masters degree in Geography with an emphasis in Geographic Information Systems. My thesis project will combine my interests in ecological science, phenology, climate, and geospatial modeling.

University of Wisconsin-Stevens Point
2015 - 2019
Bachelors [BS] - Wildlife Ecology and Management

I earned my undergraduate degree in Wildlife Ecology and Management from the University of Wisconsin-Stevens Point. My minors were completed in Biology and Geographic Information Systems.

Career Experience

My work experience over the last eight years has been in the ecological sciences and conservation GIS fields. This includes work in the academic, private, and public sectors.

University of Missouri
2023 - Present
Graduate Teaching Assistant - Geography Dept

Deliver engaging instructional sessions to undergraduate students within discussion segments of a world regional geography course, concentrating on countries across Europe, Latin America, and North America. Duties include syllabus development, assignment evaluation, and overseeing course management system for 50+ students.

Missouri Department of Conservation
2022 - 2023
Assistant Wildlife Diversity Coordinator - Wildlife Diversity Program

Collaborated with an inter-branch leadership team to implement the Missouri Bat Habitat Conservation Plan, serving as a technical lead for data reporting. Summarized large tabular datasets, analyzed spatial data from multiple enterprise geodatabases, and prepared data visualizations and summaries in RStudio and ArcGIS Pro for inclusion in federally mandated reports on statewide habitat management and bat monitoring.

Missouri Department of Conservation
2022 - 2022
Wildlife Biologist - Furbearer Program

Served in a technical role for a statewide furbearer program, contributing to large portions of the research and management of furbearing species in Missouri. Managed large-scale data sets of furbearer harvest records, public observations, and other data into multi-dimensional relational databases, utilizing GIS mapping and programmatic software. Generated and presented informative population summaries, data visualizations, and other data-driven reports for internal and external stakeholders.

Missouri Department of Conservation
2020 - 2022
Assistant Natural History Biologist - Black Bear Program

Led field and technical operations for a statewide black bear research and management program. Coordinated the monitoring of tagged black bears via ground and aerial telemetry, satellite web services, and physical capture of individuals while utilizing mobile GIS solutions for navigation and data collection. Developed and maintained black bear observation and biological databases using desktop GIS software and relational databases, ensuring the continuity of data integrity.

Research & Reporting

Data entry, management, and analysis using Program R, Microsoft Excel, Access, and SQL Server. Reproducible report design and development in Microsoft Word, R Markdown, and Quarto. Knowledge of scientific methods and research project development.

Design & Visualization
