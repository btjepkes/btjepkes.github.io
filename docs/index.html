My name is Benjamin Tjepkes and this is meum portfolium.

Experienced in ecology, delving into geography, and actively practicing geospatial – my passion lies in the realm of applied science.

Latest updates.

Annoucements

I recently started my Masters Degree within the Geography Department at the University of Missouri. I am in the process of developing my thesis project, so stay tuned for more. Please reach out if you have any inquires or would like to collaborate on any ecological or geospatial projects.

I am excited to have registered for the Posit 2023 Conference in September, where I will be attending virtually. Looking forward to learning more about open source data science. More info on posit::conf(2023) here.

One of my latest maps, Stamped in History: 50 Years of Imperiled Species Conservation, recently won 2nd place in the 2023 GIS Certification Institute (GISCI) Map Contest within the Professional category.

Dedicated scientist.

Biography

I am a wildlife ecologist and geospatial practitioner interested in advancing the science behind natural resource conservation. My work focuses on using a variety of data science and geospatial tools to research the natural world. I have over 7 years of progressive experience with governmental, private, and academic entities in the conservation and environmental science sector.
