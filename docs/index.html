A Little About Me…

Twitter Github Instagram LinkedIn ORCID iD

My Biography — I am a dedicated wildlife ecologist and geospatial practitioner interested in advancing the science behind natural resource conservation. My work focuses on using data science and geospatial techniques to research and inform landscape and endangered resource conservation. In my current role, I serve as the Assistant Wildlife Diversity Coordinator for the Missouri Department of Conservation. I will be pursing a Masters in Geography starting fall 2023, so please reach out if you would like to collaborate on a geospatial project.

Experience using radio telemetry, standardized field sampling, and wildlife capture and marking techniques to perofrm well in the field. Focus areas: Applied Conservation Science, Spatial and Movement Ecology, Imperiled Species Conservation, and Landscape Connectivity & Planning.

Data Visualization

Includes static visualizations fit for print and interactive graphics optimized for web. I apply information design best practices to synthesize your data and highlight key insights. Use cases: pitch decks, white papers, internal reports, website development.

Reports & Dashboards

Workshops & Training

I work with teams to build data-driven cultures by providing one-on-one coaching and group workshops. This includes hands-on training for data practitioners looking to elevate their visualization skills, as well as non-technical training to help business professionals improve data literacy.
